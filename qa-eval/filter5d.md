# Filter5d Analysis: Multi-Axis Relevance Scoring

The five-axis (5d) filter variant ([`answer_filter.py --verdicts
5d`](#answer_filterpy)) decomposes the per-chapter relevance judgement across
**five orthogonal axes** instead of collapsing it onto a single scale. This
document covers the design motivation, the standalone analysis script
([`filter5d.py`](#filter5dpy)), and the findings from the English run.

For the single-axis variants (Filter2/3/10/100) see [filter.md](filter.md);
for the motivation and remaining work see [PLAN.md](PLAN.md).

## The problem — the gold-scored-0 floor

The single-axis filter variants share one failure mode: a handful of gold
chapters score 0 and are unrecoverable at any threshold. **Scale granularity
did not fix this** — filter10 had a floor of 7/86, and filter100 (a finer
0–100 scale) actually grew it to 11/86 (see [filter.md](filter.md#filter100-
filter10-x10)). The model's genuine uncertainty shape is bimodal — a chapter
is either clearly relevant or clearly not — and a finer scale just lets the
model express more confidence that an indirectly-relevant chapter is
irrelevant.

The lever is not scale but **decomposition**: rate each `(chapter, question)`
pair on five independent relevance axes, each 0–10, so that a chapter scoring
0 on one axis can still earn points on another. Structurally, a chapter must
score 0 on **all five** axes to total 0 — a much higher bar than a single
snap-to-0.

| axis | captures |
|------|----------|
| `factual` | facts that directly answer the question |
| `entity` | the question's characters, objects, or places appear or act |
| `causal` | causes, prerequisites, or consequences of the question's event |
| `reference` | cross-references, callbacks, or foreshadowing linked to the question |
| `thematic` | themes, symbols, or motifs related to the question |

## `filter5d.py`

Standalone analysis script (no LLM, no output file — terminal tables only,
like [`filter.py`](filter.md#filterpy) and
[`sweep_rag.py`](README.md#sweep_ragpy)). It reads `filter5d.tsv` and the gold
chapters from `questions-<lang>.jsonl` to answer the three questions posed in
[PLAN.md](PLAN.md):

1. **The floor** — how many gold pairs land at sum=0?
2. **Sum distribution** — does a threshold separate gold from non-gold?
3. **Per-axis distribution** — which axes carry the gold signal?

Run:

```sh
make filter5d              # builds filter5d.tsv if missing, then runs this
make filter5d LANG=ja      # Japanese
```

Eight tables:

1. **Sum distribution** — histogram of 5-axis sums over all pairs, split by
   gold vs non-gold, with the gold/non-gold min/max/mean.
2. **Sum threshold sweep** — for each sum threshold 0–21: strict recall,
   partial coverage, precision, F1, avg chapters kept, and **excess** (non-gold
   chapters kept). The excess column is specific to this script's design
   posture: *absence is the failure, surfeit is not*.
3. **Per-type breakdown** — the sum sweep repeated for each gold `type`
   (`single` / `cross`).
4. **Floor and boundary gold pairs** — gold pairs at sum=0 (the floor), plus
   the 15 lowest-sum gold pairs showing which axes carry each boundary case.
5. **Per-axis score distribution** — for each axis, the count of gold and
   non-gold pairs at each score 0–10, with the gold minimum.
6. **Per-axis contribution** — for each axis: gold/non-gold counts above 0,
   noise ratio (fraction of total score going to non-gold), single-axis
   survival (keep if axis > 0), and indispensability (gold pairs lost if the
   axis is removed from the sum).
7. **Axis redundancy** — drop one axis from the sum and recompute the floor,
   gold count below sum=5, and excess at sum>0 / sum>=5. Reveals which axes
   are structurally redundant.
8. **Alternative keep rules** — `max(axis) >= threshold` (any one axis fires)
   and `non-zero-axis count >= n` (majority vote), swept across thresholds.
9. **OR combination search** — exhaustively searches per-axis threshold
   combinations (`axis_i >= t_i` for each axis, combined via OR) for the one
   that minimizes excess while preserving full gold inclusion. Reported as an
   **upper bound**, not a recommended rule — see
   [below](#or-combination-an-upper-bound-not-a-rule).

## Findings (English run)

50 questions, 37 chapters, 1,850 `(chapter, question)` pairs, 86 of them gold.
Generated by `make filter5d`.

### The floor: eliminated

**0 of 86 gold pairs land at sum=0** — the design goal is fully achieved. The
five-axis decomposition structurally prevents the all-zero verdict that
single-axis scoring collapses to on indirectly-relevant chapters.

| variant | gold pairs at score/sum 0 | floor |
|---------|---------------------------|------:|
| Filter10 (single 0–10) | 7 / 86 | 8.1% |
| Filter100 (single 0–100) | 11 / 86 | 12.8% |
| **Filter5d (five-axis sum)** | **0 / 86** | **0%** |

No single axis is individually indispensable for this floor: removing any one
axis from the sum still yields 0 gold pairs at sum=0 (Table 7). Every gold pair
has signal on at least two axes, so the floor survives the loss of any one.

### Sum distribution: a natural separation at sum=5

The gold minimum sum is **5** — no gold pair scores below it — while 615
non-gold pairs occupy the sum 1–4 band. This creates a clean separator:

| keep rule | strict recall | excess | avg kept |
|-----------|--------------:|-------:|---------:|
| sum > 0 (≥ 1) | 1.000 | 1244 | 26.6 |
| **sum ≥ 5** | **1.000** | **629** | **14.3** |
| sum ≥ 6 | 0.960 | 547 | 12.6 |

`sum ≥ 5` preserves all gold while cutting excess roughly in half versus the
binarized `sum > 0` rule. At `sum ≥ 6` the first gold pairs drop (Q42 Ch23 and
Q50 Ch23, both at sum=5). The boundary pair Q42 Ch23 (entity=1, causal=2,
reference=1, thematic=1) is the hardest case — its signal is spread thin across
four axes, none above 2.

The separation is type-dependent: `single` questions maintain strict recall
1.000 up to sum=21 (their gold chapter always scores very high), while `cross`
questions hit the ceiling at sum=5 and start dropping at sum=6.

### Per-axis analysis: who carries the gold?

The five axes have sharply different signal-to-noise profiles (Table 6):

| axis | gold > 0 | non-gold > 0 | noise ratio | role |
|------|---------:|-------------:|------------:|------|
| `factual` | 46/86 | 13 | **0.12** | precision anchor (but redundant in sum) |
| `entity` | **86/86** | 1209 | 0.84 | recall driver (catches everything, noisy) |
| `causal` | 84/86 | 446 | 0.69 | balanced |
| `reference` | 76/86 | 450 | 0.78 | underperforms (10 gold at 0) |
| `thematic` | 85/86 | 826 | 0.83 | noisy |

**`entity` is the only axis where gold min > 0** (min=1). Every gold pair has
at least entity=1 — the question's characters, objects, or places always appear
in a gold chapter. This makes `entity ≥ 1` the only single-axis rule that
achieves full gold inclusion, though at huge excess (1209 non-gold pairs).

**`factual` is the most precise** (noise ratio 0.12 — 88% of its total score
goes to gold) but catches only 46/86 gold pairs. The 40 gold pairs at factual=0
are cross-reference chapters that don't directly answer the question on their
own — the answer requires combining information from multiple chapters.

### `factual` is redundant in the sum

Dropping `factual` from the five-axis sum changes **nothing**: the floor stays
0/86, the excess at sum>0 stays 1244, and the excess at sum≥5 stays 629 (Table
7, identical to the full 5-axis baseline). Every pair with factual>0 also has
signal on at least one other axis, so factual adds no discriminative power to
the sum rule.

This is a structural property of the axis: a chapter that directly answers the
question (factual>0) necessarily contains the question's entities, themes, and
causal context, so the other axes already fire. Factual is subsumed.

That said, factual remains valuable as a **precision gate** if used
independently — only 13 non-gold pairs have factual>0, so a rule like
`factual ≥ 6` is near-pure gold (though it misses the cross-reference half).

### `reference` underperforms expectations

The `reference` axis was designed to rescue cross-reference chapters — the
indirectly-relevant callbacks and foreshadowing that single-axis scoring drops.
In practice, **10 gold pairs score reference=0**: these are chapters whose
relevance is causal or thematic rather than callback-based. `causal` (84/86
gold above 0) and `thematic` (85/86) rescue more gold than `reference` (76/86).

### Keep-rule recommendation

**`sum ≥ 5`** is the recommended keep rule for Phase 2. It matches the gold
minimum sum exactly, giving a clean separator that:

- Preserves all 86 gold pairs (strict recall 1.000).
- Halves the excess versus `sum > 0` (629 vs 1244 non-gold chapters).
- Requires no per-axis threshold tuning (unlike an OR combination — see
  [below](#or-combination-an-upper-bound-not-a-rule)).

The simpler `sum > 0` rule is equally valid if excess is not a concern (the
design posture is "surfeit costs only Phase 2 tokens, not correctness") — the
Ceiling run at 0.990 confirms that the answer model can synthesize correctly
from large chapter sets, so over-inclusion does not hurt accuracy, only cost.

### OR combination: an upper bound, not a rule

Table 9 exhaustively searches per-axis threshold combinations — "keep if
`axis_i >= t_i` for any axis i" — for the combination that minimizes excess
while preserving full gold inclusion (each axis threshold swept 1–5, 5^5 =
3,125 combinations). The best result:

| rule | excess | avg kept |
|------|-------:|---------:|
| **Best OR**: `entity≥3 OR causal≥2 OR reference≥5 OR thematic≥4` | **613** | **14.0** |
| Baseline: `sum ≥ 5` | 629 | 14.3 |

The OR rule trims 16 excess pairs (2.5%) versus `sum ≥ 5`. The top-5
combinations are all tied at excess=613 with `factual` ranging `≥1` through
`≥5` — factual's threshold is irrelevant because it is structurally redundant
(see [factual is redundant](#factual-is-redundant-in-the-sum) above).

**This is not a recommended rule.** The thresholds are fitted to the known gold
set: the search picks the combination that happens to minimize excess across
these exact 86 gold pairs, after seeing the answer. Five thresholds chosen from
3,125 candidates over 86 data points is **fitting the metric, not learning a
generalizable rule** — the specific thresholds (`reference≥5`, `thematic≥4`)
are driven by 2–3 boundary gold pairs (Q42 Ch23 at entity=1, Q32 Ch15 at
entity=2), and a different question set would produce different "optimal"
thresholds.

The simpler `sum ≥ 5` rule achieves the same gold inclusion with 2.5% more
excess and **zero degrees of freedom** — one threshold on one derived quantity,
not five independently tuned per-axis gates. That robustness is worth the 16
excess pairs.

### Comparison with Filter10

| metric | Filter10 (≥ 3) | Filter5d (sum ≥ 5) |
|--------|---------------:|-------------------:|
| strict recall | 0.760 | **1.000** |
| partial recall | 0.857 | **1.000** |
| precision | 0.788 | 0.120 |
| avg kept | 1.7 | 14.3 |
| gold floor | 7/86 | **0/86** |

Filter5d trades precision for recall: it keeps ~14 chapters per question
versus Filter10's ~2, but catches every gold chapter. The precision drop is
expected — the design goal was to eliminate the floor, not to match Filter10's
precision. The Ceiling result (0.990) confirms that over-inclusion does not
hurt answer accuracy, so the recall/precision trade-off favors recall here.
